## Section 1 — Two Operating Models (canonical UI mental model)

CHL supports two distinct operating models that the operator selects per-load (or per-shipper relationship):

### Model A — CHL factors its OWN shipper invoices ("broker-factor")

CHL sells its own outbound shipper invoices to a broker-factor (HaulPay, Saint John, Apex, Triumph, RTS, OTR, eCapital, etc.) for Day-1 cash. The carrier may factor independently (their choice).

- **Cash flow:** factor advances 80-95% (recourse) / 92-96% (non-recourse) of broker invoice within 24h of POD acceptance.
- **Reserve:** 5-10% recourse / 0% true non-recourse, released when shipper pays factor on Net-30/60.
- **Factor fee:** 1.5-5% (central band 2-4%).
- **NOA direction:** broker→shipper (UCC § 9-406; GGG3 outbound stamp).
- **Use case:** velocity-of-loads + working-capital-shock-absorber for solo brokerage.

### Model B — Carrier factors only; CHL collects shipper Net-30 directly ("driver-factor-only")

Only the carrier factors their own invoice. CHL collects the shipper directly on Net-30.

- **Cash flow:** CHL invoices shipper, waits 30+ days, collects via Mercury hosted-payment link.
- **NOA direction:** carrier→broker only (DDD4 inbound detection); CHL does NOT issue outbound NOA.
- **Risk profile:** CHL is the bank — credit-limit discipline at shipper onboarding is the protection lever.
- **Dunning:** Day 7 / 25 / 31 automated sequence.
- **Use case:** repeat shippers with proven AP discipline; higher net margin (no factor fee leakage).

The two models share Phases 1-4 (sourcing → vet → book → dispatch → in-transit → POD); they diverge at Phase 5 (Invoicing & Settlement).

## Section 2 — End-to-End Stages

The 14 stage cards below represent CHL's canonical workflow. Each stage maps to: a UI tab, an applicability path, a list of required actions, the code surface that automates it, regulatory citations where relevant, decision points, and audit/observability surfaces.

**Path tags:**
- `BOTH PATHS` — applies to Model A + Model B

### Stage 1 — Lead / Shipper Onboarding + Factor Pick

**UI tab:** Required Tasks · Factor Picker · Credit Lookup (Model A) · Shipper Portal · Compliance · Vault (Model B)
**Path:** `BOTH PATHS` (Model A focus); `DIRECT SHIPPER ONLY` (Model B onboarding pre-quote)
**Automation:** AUTONOMOUS (RFQ parsing + factor picker prompt creation) + OPERATOR-ASSISTED (factor selection + shipper credit decision)

**Required actions:**

- (Model A) Direct-shipper RFQ: AI-parsed RFQ lands in Required Tasks → Factor Picker modal opens
- (Model A) Load-board path: parse listing, grab poster's MC#, open Factor Picker on load detail
- (Model A) Pick from 15 wired factors (HaulPay, Saint John, Apex, Triumph, RTS, OTR, eCapital, +9 more)
- (Model A) Selected factor locks onto RFQ + every quote row + the eventual load + the eventual invoice/NOA
- (Model A) Run factor's Credit Lookup on poster's MC# (each factor maintains its own approved-shipper DB)
- (Model A) Approved → proceed. Denied → re-pick factor or skip the load.
- (Model B) Capture shipper: legal name, billing address, AP contact + email, phone, Tax ID
- (Model B) Pull shipper credit report (Ansonia / RTS / D&B) → set credit limit + Net-30 default
- (Model B) E-sign Master Broker-Shipper Agreement + store in Compliance Vault

**Code surface:**
- `backend/load_intake.py` — RFQ ingestion + AI parse
- `backend/factor_router.py` — factor-pick lock-in
- `backend/shipper_credit_lookup.py` — factor credit-API integration
- `chl-memory/operations/shipper_broker_agreement_template_2026_05_09.md` — Model B agreement template

**Regulatory:** § 371.3(a)(1) consignor capture (load_schema_v2.py:40-41) · § 371.9(a) COI representation (template, pending agreement build)

**Decision:** factor approves vs. denies → escalation: re-pick factor / skip load / call shipper for AP contact correction.

**Observability:** factor decision logged to `db.factor_decisions`; shipper credit pulls logged for FCRA defense.

### Stage 5 — Vet the Carrier (CRITICAL — fraud-defense gate)

**UI tab:** Carriers DB / Vault
**Path:** `BOTH PATHS`
**Automation:** AUTONOMOUS fraud filters + OPERATOR-ASSISTED final approval

**Why this is non-skippable:** negligent-selection liability lives here. A skipped vet on a load that goes wrong = a personal-injury attorney's first-best target. Even repeat carriers must be re-vetted (insurance lapses silently).

**Required actions:**

- FMCSA SAFER: active authority, OOS rate, insurance — `carrier_safer_check.py`
- Pull current COI — broker named as cert holder — `coi_validator.py`
- Carrier Agreement + W-9 + encrypted banking — `broker_carrier_agreement.py`, `vault.py`
- Carrier411 / RMIS check (negligent-selection guard) — **SIGNAL only, not gate** (legal exposure: $458k defamation verdict Oct 2023)

**Fraud-defense layers (all autonomous):**

- **DDD1 — Authority age + phone-FMCSA cross-check** (`carrier_fraud_filters.py`). Authority < 90 days = CHL-policy red flag (NOT industry rule). Phone mismatch with FMCSA registry = fraud filter trip.
- **GGG7 — ELD-vs-fleet-size discrepancy** (`eld_fleet_discrepancy_check.py`). Carrier claims 30 trucks; only 3 ELDs report → fraud signal.
- **GGG2 — Continuous carrier monitoring** (`carrier_continuous_monitor.py`, CRON 30 daily). Re-vet between loads: insurance lapse, authority change, ownership change, ELD count change.
- **GGG9 — Contact enrichment adapter framework** (`carrier_contact_enrichment.py`). Hunter / PDL / SOS-Cobalt sources for verified contact (privacy policy + DPAs prerequisite).

**Code surface:**
- `backend/carrier_vetting_workflow.py` (orchestrator)
- `backend/carrier_fraud_filters.py` (DDD1)
- `backend/eld_fleet_discrepancy_check.py` (GGG7)
- `backend/carrier_continuous_monitor.py` (GGG2)
- `backend/carrier_contact_enrichment.py` (GGG9)
- `backend/coi_validator.py`
- `backend/vault.py` (encrypted banking storage)

**Regulatory:** § 371.2 broker-status framing (TIA Model BCA template); negligent-selection common-law standard.

**Decision:** PASS → Stage 6 · CONDITIONAL (operator review queue) · FAIL (fraud filter trip) → escalate to `urgent_alerts`.
