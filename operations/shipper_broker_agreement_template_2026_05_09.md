## 1. Representations & Warranties -- Broker Status (49 CFR sec 371 compliance clause)

> **Operator note:** This is the load-bearing clause for the HHH2 / HHH1
> Gap #3 remediation. The clause discloses CHL's broker status per
> 49 CFR sec 371.7(b), addresses sec 371.9(a) self-dealing concerns by
> stating CHL has no shipper ownership stake, and clarifies cargo-claim
> routing per 49 USC sec 14706 (Carmack Amendment) so Shipper does not
> mistakenly assume CHL carries cargo liability.

CONTINENTAL HAUL LOGISTICS LLC ("CHL") is a duly licensed PROPERTY
BROKER under FMCSA Authority MC-1817555 / USDOT-4569843. CHL is NOT:

- A motor carrier
- An indirect carrier
- A freight forwarder
- A common carrier of any class

CHL's role under this Agreement is to arrange transportation of
Shipper's property by motor carriers selected by CHL in its capacity as
broker. CHL does not take possession, custody, or control of Shipper's
cargo at any time.

Cargo claims arising from loss, damage, delay, or shortage shall be
pursued by Shipper directly against the carrier whose Bill of Lading
covers the shipment, not against CHL. Shipper acknowledges that the
Carmack Amendment (49 USC sec 14706) governs cargo claims against
motor carriers and that CHL's role is non-carrier in that regulatory
framework.

CHL is at all times acting in its broker capacity per 49 CFR Part 371.
CHL does not represent that it has carrier-level cargo liability or
insurance, and Shipper acknowledges this distinction.

### 1.a Conflict-of-Interest Representation (49 CFR sec 371.9(a))

Shipper represents and warrants that:

(i) No officer, director, member, manager, or beneficial owner holding
    five percent (5%) or more of Shipper has any material beneficial
    ownership interest in CHL;
(ii) Shipper does not own or control CHL through common ownership of
     any kind; and
(iii) CHL does not own or control Shipper through common ownership of
      any kind.

CHL represents and warrants that:

(i) No officer, director, member, manager, or beneficial owner holding
    five percent (5%) or more of CHL has any material beneficial
    ownership interest in Shipper; and
(ii) CHL does not own or hold a material beneficial interest in any
     property tendered for transportation under this Agreement.

If any of the foregoing representations becomes inaccurate during the
term of this Agreement, the disclosing Party shall give written notice
to the other Party within ten (10) business days of becoming aware,
and the Parties shall in good faith determine whether continued
performance under this Agreement complies with 49 CFR sec 371.9(a).

### 1.b Anti-Rebating Acknowledgment (49 CFR sec 371.9(b))

The Parties acknowledge that 49 CFR sec 371.9(b) prohibits CHL from
giving Shipper (or its officers or employees) anything of value beyond
"inexpensive advertising items given for promotional purposes." CHL's
internal Promotional-Items Policy (current version: 2026-05-09) caps
items at $25 per item and $50 per recipient per calendar year, and
prohibits cash + cash-equivalent gifts of any amount. This Agreement
does not modify, supersede, or expand the regulatory prohibition.

## 8. Records & Audit Right (49 CFR sec 371.3 acknowledgement)

CHL maintains brokerage records per 49 CFR sec 371.3 for a minimum of
seven (7) years (exceeding the regulatory three-year floor as a
conservative industry standard). Each Party to a brokered transaction
has the right to review the record of that transaction per
49 CFR sec 371.3(c). To exercise this right, Shipper may submit a
records request via CHL's records-request endpoint at
[https://continentalhaul.com/records-request] (or via written request
to dispatch@continentalhaul.com); CHL targets a forty-eight-hour (48h)
response window.

This Agreement does not waive Shipper's right to review the records of
any transaction. Any term of this Agreement that purports to waive
this right is void and unenforceable.
