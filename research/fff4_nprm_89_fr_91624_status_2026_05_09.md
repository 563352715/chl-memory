## 3. Proposed-rule text (NPRM 89 FR 91648, Nov 20 2024)

### 3.1 Current 49 CFR 371.3 baseline (pre-NPRM, still in force)

Quoted from eCFR (S7/S8):

> **§ 371.3 Records to be kept by brokers.**
>
> (a) A broker shall keep a record of each transaction. For purposes of this section, brokers may keep master lists of consignors and the address and registration number of the carrier, rather than repeating this information for each transaction. The record shall show:
>   (1) The name and address of the consignor;
>   (2) The name, address, and registration number of the originating motor carrier;
>   (3) The bill of lading or freight bill number;
>   (4) The amount of compensation received by the broker for the brokerage service performed and the name of the payer;
>   (5) A description of any non-brokerage service performed in connection with each shipment or other activity, the amount of compensation received for the service, and the name of the payer; and
>   (6) The amount of any freight charges collected by the broker and the date of payment to the carrier.
>
> (b) Brokers shall keep the records required by this section for a period of three years.
>
> (c) Each party to a brokered transaction has the right to review the record of the transaction required to be kept by these rules.

### 3.2 NPRM proposed amendments (per S4, S11, S15, S17 — all paraphrasing; verbatim verification pending)

The NPRM proposes **four major changes**:

**Change #1 -- Electronic recordkeeping mandate.**
[Per S4 / S15]: "Brokers would be required to keep their transaction records for each shipment in an electronic format in order to facilitate more efficient access." The NPRM does not narrowly define "electronic format" -- preamble discussion (per S15) leaves brokers latitude on file format, but the records must be machine-readable + transmittable electronically (i.e., paper-only is non-compliant; PDF / structured DB / spreadsheet all qualify). Verbatim text pending S3 retrieval.

**Change #2 -- Modernized + expanded record contents.**
[Per S15]: Records under § 371.3(a) would be expanded to include comprehensive transaction details for each shipment, including:
- Documentation of all charges and payments
- Descriptions of all charges and services (brokerage + non-brokerage)
- Cargo claims documentation
- Identity of the parties to the transaction (carrier, shipper, consignee)
- The bill of lading / freight bill / pro number

Verbatim list of fields pending S3 retrieval.

**Change #3 -- Reframed as regulatory duty (waiver ban).**
[Per S11 verbatim quote-of-quote]: "FMCSA proposes to reframe broker transparency as a regulatory duty imposed on brokers to provide records to the transacting parties, rather than as a right held by carriers and shippers that must be exercised through a request." The practical effect is a **statutory bar on contractual waivers** of the carrier's record-access right. Per S15, current rule § 371.3(c) creates a right that carriers can (and per TIA, do) waive in master broker-carrier contracts; the NPRM converts this from a waivable right to an unwaivable obligation on the broker.

**Change #4 -- 48-hour disclosure on request.**
[Per S15, S17, S19]: "Brokers shall provide the records required to be maintained under § 371.3(a) within 48 hours when a party to the transaction requests those records." Per S15, OOIDA had requested **automatic** delivery within 48 hours of completion; FMCSA scaled this back to **on-request** delivery within 48 hours. The 48-hour clock starts on receipt of request; the NPRM does not specify business vs calendar hours [pending S3 verification].

### 3.3 What the NPRM does NOT propose (key delta from OOIDA petition)

- Does NOT mandate **automatic** post-delivery disclosure (carrier still must request).
- Does NOT define a specific **electronic file format** (PDF, EDI, JSON all permissible per preamble discussion in S15).
- Does NOT specify **penalties** beyond existing 49 USC 14901 / 14906 broker-misconduct civil penalty structure.
- Does NOT change the **3-year retention** period in current § 371.3(b).
- Does NOT modify **49 CFR 371.7** (misrepresentation) -- that's untouched.

### 3.4 NPRM proposed effective date

The NPRM did not propose a specific effective date or compliance date. Per FMCSA standard practice (per S15), expect:
- **Effective date:** 60 days after final rule publication
- **Compliance date:** 6-12 months after effective date for substantive process changes

This is conjecture for sNPRM/final-rule planning -- final-rule effective+compliance dates will be set in the final rule preamble.

## 5. Implementation requirements for CHL records architecture

Treating the NPRM as the **floor** (final rule will be at least this strict, possibly stricter), CHL must architect for:

### 5.1 Per-shipment transaction record (canonical fields)

Minimum data captured per load/transaction in CHL's database:

```
transaction_record:
  load_id                       (PK)
  consignor_name + address      (proposed § 371.3(a)(1))
  origin_carrier_name           (proposed § 371.3(a)(2))
  origin_carrier_address
  origin_carrier_mc_or_dot      (registration number)
  bol_number                    (proposed § 371.3(a)(3))
  freight_bill_number           (alt to BOL)
  pro_number                    (alt)
  brokerage_compensation_amt    (proposed § 371.3(a)(4))
  brokerage_compensation_payer
  non_brokerage_services        [array of {description, amount, payer}]  (proposed § 371.3(a)(5))
  freight_charges_collected_amt (proposed § 371.3(a)(6))
  carrier_payment_date
  carrier_payment_amt
  cargo_claim_documentation     [array of refs]  (NPRM expansion)
  shipper_name + address        (NPRM expansion -- formerly only "consignor")
  consignee_name + address      (NPRM expansion)
  all_charges_and_services      [array of {description, amount, payer, type}]
  created_at
  updated_at
```

CHL's existing `shipments` / `load_settlements` tables likely cover most of this; gap-analysis is a separate ticket.

### 5.2 Format requirements

- **Electronic = required.** No paper-only records anywhere in CHL workflow. Already true (CHL is database-native).
- **Machine-readable.** Records must be retrievable as structured data, not as image-scanned PDFs. CHL's Postgres tables satisfy.
- **Per-transaction granularity.** No "master list" optimization for shipper/carrier identity is permitted under NPRM Change #2. Each load must carry full party identity (CHL already does this).

### 5.3 48-hour disclosure pipeline

Required new capability (per Change #4):
1. **Request intake endpoint** -- party to the transaction (carrier OR shipper, both have standing) submits a request for records on a specific load.
2. **Identity verification** -- requester must be a "party to the transaction" (carrier MC or shipper account-of-record). Reject third-party requests.
3. **Records assembly** -- assemble all § 371.3(a) fields for the load.
4. **Delivery within 48 hours** -- electronic transmission (email PDF + structured CSV; signed link to portal also acceptable).
5. **Audit log** -- capture (request_id, requester_party, load_id, requested_at, delivered_at, delivery_channel, hash_of_records_delivered) for FMCSA audit.

**Recommended SLA:** 24 hours target with 48-hour hard deadline + alert if approaching.
**Hours measurement:** Until final rule clarifies, treat 48 hours as **calendar hours** (most conservative) from receipt of request. If request received at 3pm Friday, deliver by 3pm Sunday. If final rule carves out weekends/holidays, relax later.

### 5.4 Waiver-ban implications for CHL contracts

CHL's standard broker-carrier agreement template MUST NOT contain any provision that:
- Waives the carrier's right to access transaction records
- Conditions record access on signing an NDA broader than the records themselves
- Requires the carrier to pay a "records request fee" (current TIA model agreement contains this — need to strike)
- Imposes a blackout period during which records are unavailable

**Action item for CHL:** Audit `templates/broker_carrier_agreement.md` and `templates/carrier_packet/*` against the NPRM's waiver-ban provision. EEE1 already flagged the TIA model agreement (regulations.gov FMCSA-2020-0190-0072 attachment 3) contains a waiver -- if CHL inherited any waiver language from that template, strip it.

### 5.5 Penalty exposure (current)

Even without the new rule, current § 371.3 records non-compliance is enforceable under 49 USC 14901 (civil penalties up to $11,463 per violation, indexed for inflation -- 2024 amount) and 49 USC 14906 (broker misconduct). The NEW rule would not change penalty caps absent explicit per-day language; it would change the **enforceability** -- removing the waiver-ban means brokers can no longer hide behind contractual waivers.

## 6. Industry response

### 6.1 OOIDA (carrier side -- supportive)

[Per S10, S18]: "Strongly supports" the NPRM. OOIDA filed reply comments asking FMCSA to **strengthen** the rule by:
- Restoring **automatic** post-delivery disclosure (not just on-request)
- Adding specific civil penalties per violation
- Tightening the 48-hour clock to "1 business day"

OOIDA quote (per S10): *"It's time to level the playing field. It's time for broker transparency."*

### 6.2 SBTC (small carrier side -- supportive, requested extension)

Co-petitioner with OOIDA. Filed comments supporting the rule and requested the comment-period extension that closed 2025-03-20 (S5).

### 6.3 TIA (broker side -- opposed)

[Per S9, S10]: TIA "deeply disappointed." Filed formal comments arguing:
- Existing § 371.3 is "outdated, unnecessary and burdensome"
- Rule is "a solution in search of a problem"
- FMCSA was **not authorized by Congress** to issue the rule (signaling future APA / ultra-vires lawsuit if final rule issues with these provisions)
- FMCSA should focus on freight fraud (~$1B/yr) instead of broker transparency

TIA's prior posture (2020 petition): asked FMCSA to **delete** § 371.3 entirely. Their NPRM comments reiterate this.

### 6.4 ATA (large-carrier-trade side -- opposed)

[Per S10]: Opposes the NPRM. Argues:
- Requirements would create "undue strain" on brokers (their brokerage members)
- FMCSA exceeded statutory authority

### 6.5 Litigation risk

**No lawsuit filed yet** (final rule has not published; nothing to challenge). However, TIA has explicitly signaled (S9) they will challenge if a final rule retains the waiver-ban. Expected legal theories:
1. **Major Questions Doctrine** -- broker-carrier contract regulation is an "elephant in a mousehole" not clearly authorized by 49 USC 14122 / 49 USC 13501.
2. **APA arbitrary-and-capricious** -- inadequate cost-benefit; already-low compliance with current § 371.3(c) was driven by waivers, not by recordkeeping deficiencies.
3. **First Amendment** (less likely) -- compelled disclosure of confidential commercial information.

Mitigation for CHL: even if TIA wins a vacatur, the underlying § 371.3 baseline persists, and CHL's records architecture would still meet the baseline. Build for the proposal; if final rule is vacated, the architecture is "harmless overhead." Net cost: low.

### 6.6 Major broker compliance practice (current state)

[Per S11, S15, S17 -- inferential, no T1/T2 source for individual broker policies]:
- **CH Robinson, XPO, Coyote, TQL, Echo:** All contractually waive § 371.3(c) record-access rights in their carrier master agreements. This is the practice the NPRM is designed to eliminate.
- **Convoy (defunct), Uber Freight, Loadsmart, Transfix:** Digital-native brokers. Records are already electronic. Disclosure-on-request is routine in their compliance/audit teams. Closer to NPRM-ready.
- **Compliance gap CHL must close:** None of the majors currently provide records in 48 hours on request -- typical industry practice is 5-10 business days. The 48-hour SLA is the binding new requirement, not the recordkeeping.

## 7. CHL recommended action

### 7.1 Does CHL's records architecture meet the rule today?

**Partially.** Strengths and gaps:

| Requirement | CHL current state | Compliance |
|---|---|---|
| Electronic records | Postgres native, no paper. | YES |
| § 371.3(a) field set | `shipments` + `load_settlements` tables capture most; need audit. | LIKELY (verify) |
| 3-year retention | Production DB doesn't auto-purge; verify backup retention. | LIKELY YES |
| Disclosure on request | No formal request-intake endpoint or SLA. | NO -- gap |
| 48-hour delivery | No SLA or alert. | NO -- gap |
| Waiver-free agreements | Templates may inherit TIA waiver language. | UNKNOWN -- audit needed |
| Audit log of disclosures | No dedicated log table. | NO -- gap |

### 7.2 Top 3 things CHL must build (in priority order)

**1. Records-disclosure request endpoint + 48-hour pipeline.**
- New table: `broker_records_requests` (request_id, requester_party_id, requester_role [carrier|shipper], load_id, requested_at, fulfilled_at, delivery_channel, records_hash, status).
- New endpoint: `POST /api/v1/broker-records/request` (party-authenticated; carriers via MC, shippers via account).
- Async worker: assemble § 371.3(a) record bundle, deliver via email + signed portal link, log delivery.
- Alert: any request unfulfilled at 24h triggers PagerDuty / DM @pm-lead.
- Effort: 3-5 days.

**2. Records-bundle assembly query (covers § 371.3(a) field set).**
- One canonical SQL/ORM view that joins `shipments` + `load_settlements` + `carrier_payments` + `non_brokerage_charges` + `cargo_claims` and returns the full § 371.3(a) record for any load_id.
- Output formats: JSON (API), CSV (machine-readable), PDF (human-readable).
- Effort: 2-3 days.

**3. Contract template audit + waiver scrub.**
- Audit `templates/broker_carrier_agreement*` and any onboarding packet docs for waiver language.
- Strike any clause that conditions, waives, or limits the carrier's right to records under § 371.3(c).
- Add an explicit affirmative clause: *"Carrier retains all rights under 49 CFR § 371.3 to review the broker's record of the transaction. CHL shall provide such records within 48 hours of a written request from Carrier."*
- Effort: 0.5-1 day legal review.

**Total effort to NPRM-ready:** ~6-9 dev-days + 1 legal-review day.

### 7.3 Sequencing recommendation

Build NOW (do not wait for final rule):
- Items #1, #2, #3 above. Even under current § 371.3, CHL must respond to records requests; building the pipeline preemptively eliminates manual operator-touch and aligns with platform north-star (autonomous + observable).

Defer until final rule:
- Specific format mandates (if any) -- final rule may require specific schema.
- Penalty-tracking dashboards -- pending whether final rule adds explicit per-violation penalties.
- Auto-disclosure (post-delivery push) -- only build if final rule restores OOIDA's automatic-disclosure ask.

### 7.4 Watch items (re-trigger this research)

- **Q3 2026:** Watch reginfo.gov (RIN 2126-AC63) for sNPRM publication. Expected May 2026 per S6/S14, but FMCSA cadence is unreliable.
- **6 months after sNPRM:** Comment period close. Expect final-rule cadence ~12 months after that.
- **TIA vs FMCSA litigation docket:** If TIA files a pre-enforcement challenge after final-rule publication, monitor D.C. Circuit docket.

## 8. Summary -- one-paragraph

NPRM 89 FR 91648 (corrected from brief's "91624"), Docket FMCSA-2023-0257, RIN 2126-AC63, was published Nov 20 2024 with comment periods closing Mar 20 2025. As of 2026-05-09, **no final rule has published**; OMB Spring 2025 Unified Agenda projects a **second NPRM in May 2026**, then comment period, then final rule -- so CHL has 12-24 months runway. The proposal would amend 49 CFR § 371.3 to (1) mandate electronic records, (2) expand record-content requirements, (3) ban contractual waivers, and (4) require 48-hour disclosure on party request. OOIDA + SBTC support; TIA + ATA oppose and have signaled litigation. CHL's database-native architecture already satisfies the electronic-records floor; the binding gaps are (a) a records-request intake endpoint with 48-hour SLA, (b) a canonical § 371.3(a) record-bundle assembly, and (c) a contract-template waiver scrub. Build all three NOW -- they're "harmless overhead" if TIA wins vacatur, and load-bearing if final rule issues as proposed.
