# FMCSA Regulatory Workflow for Property Brokers — End-to-End

**Researched:** 2026-05-10
**Scope:** Definitive step-by-step list of what a U.S. property broker (MC authority) is *regulated* to do — and in what order — from authority grant through load delivery and payment, per FMCSA + 49 CFR.
**Sources:** 49 CFR Part 371 (Brokers of Property), 49 CFR Part 365 (Applications), 49 CFR Part 366 (Process Agent), 49 CFR Part 387 Subpart C (Financial Responsibility), FMCSA Small Entity Compliance Guide for Broker Operations, FMCSA Broker Registration guidance pages, FMCSA Docket FMCSA-2023-0257 (Transparency NPRM, Nov 2024).
**Convention:** **[REGULATED]** = cited regulation requires it. **[CUSTOM]** = industry practice, regulation silent. **[BEST PRACTICE]** = recommended, not mandated.

---

## Part 1 — Direct answers to the seven specific questions

1. **First regulatory step after authority is granted:** The broker may not begin operations until the FMCSA *issues* the license — and the license is only issued after (a) **surety bond/trust fund (BMC-84 or BMC-85) for $75,000 is on file** [49 CFR §387.307(a)], (b) **Form BOC-3 designating process agents for every state** is on file [49 CFR §366.3 / §366.4], and (c) the **10-day protest period from FMCSA Register publication** has elapsed without protest [49 CFR Part 365]. Therefore the FIRST regulated *operational* step once the license is officially active is to begin keeping the transaction record required by §371.3 from the very first brokered shipment. There is no separate "post-grant filing" before transacting — the bond, BOC-3, and registration name must already match before authority is even granted (FMCSA Broker Registration guidance: "Legal Business Name on OP-1 must match insurance documents, Form BOC-3, and USDOT Number record").

2. **Documents required per §371.3:** A record of each transaction containing: (1) name and address of the consignor; (2) name, address, and registration number of the originating motor carrier; (3) bill of lading or freight bill number; (4) amount of compensation received by the broker and name of the payer; (5) description of any non-brokerage service, compensation, and payer; (6) amount of freight charges collected by the broker and date paid to the carrier. Master lists of consignors and carriers are permitted (no repetition per transaction). **Records are created at the moment a brokered shipment occurs** — fields (1)-(3) are typically captured at dispatch (rate confirmation + BOL issuance), field (4) at broker invoice, field (5) anywhere along the workflow, field (6) at carrier-payment settlement.

3. **Broker ↔ carrier exchanged documents — *regulated* vs *custom*:** Regulation is largely silent on broker–carrier contract documents. **[REGULATED]:** the broker's registration number, the carrier's registration number, and the freight charges/payment date (captured into the broker's §371.3 record). **[REGULATED, indirect]:** carrier must be an "authorized motor carrier" — broker must verify carrier authority is active (implied by §371.2 definition of "broker" — arranging via an *authorized* motor carrier). **[CUSTOM, near-universal]:** broker–carrier agreement (master contract), per-load rate confirmation, carrier insurance certificates, carrier W-9. None of these specific documents is named in Part 371.

4. **Broker ↔ shipper exchanged documents — *regulated* vs *custom*:** **[REGULATED]:** §371.3(c) — "Each party to a brokered transaction has the right to review the record of the transaction required to be kept by these rules." This is the only mandated document *exchange* obligation toward the shipper at the federal level. **[REGULATED, conditional]:** §371.7 — broker must not represent itself as a carrier (so any document exchanged must accurately disclose broker status). **[CUSTOM]:** broker-shipper master agreement, credit application, rate quote, broker invoice, proof of delivery. The shipper-side BOL is a carrier-shipper instrument, not a broker document — the broker captures the BOL *number* per §371.3(a)(3) but is not regulated to produce it.

5. **Credit-checking — regulated?** **No federal regulation requires credit-checking** of either the carrier or the shipper. There is no FMCSA mandate to run credit on a shipper or to confirm a carrier's payment history. Carrier *safety* and *authority* verification is implied by §371.2 (must be an authorized motor carrier). Credit vetting is **[BEST PRACTICE]** driven by surety-bond claim risk and the broker's own commercial exposure — not regulation. Note: a separate regulation (FMCSA 2010 final rule under MAP-21 §32918) requires the **surety/trust** to be in place to *cover* unpaid carrier claims — but does not require the broker to credit-check shippers.

6. **Record retention period:** **3 years**, confirmed per **49 CFR §371.3(d)** — "Brokers shall keep the records required by this section for a period of three years." Each party to the transaction has the right to review the record within that period [§371.3(c)].

7. **Carrier-rate disclosure to shipper — scope + timing:** §371.3(c) gives each party "the right to review the record" — this is a **disclosure-on-request** obligation, not a proactive pre-load disclosure. Specifically, the record (which by §371.3(a)(4) includes broker compensation and by §371.3(a)(6) includes freight charges to the carrier) must be made *available for review* by any party to the transaction. The shipper, the motor carrier, and the broker are all "parties" to the same brokered transaction (per FMCSA Docket FMCSA-2023-0257 preamble). **Timing:** today, on request, within commercially reasonable time. **Pending (NPRM, Nov 20, 2024 — Docket FMCSA-2023-0257):** proposed amendments would (a) require electronic records, (b) mandate production within 48 hours of request, (c) reframe disclosure as a non-waivable regulatory obligation. Not yet final as of this writing — comments closed Jan 21, 2025; rule status remains proposed.

## Part 2 — Step-by-step ordered list of every regulated action

### Phase A — Authority Establishment (one-time, pre-operational)

**Step 1. File application for broker authority via URS.** [REGULATED — 49 CFR §365.105, §365.107]
- Form OP-1 (new applicants since Dec 12, 2015 file via Unified Registration System; OP-1 reserved for additional-authority filings).
- Counterparty: FMCSA.
- Fee: $300 non-refundable.
- Output: USDOT number assigned; MC docket number historically issued but as of Oct 1, 2025, MC numbers are no longer issued — USDOT number is sole identifier.

**Step 2. File surety bond OR trust fund.** [REGULATED — 49 CFR §387.307(a), (b)]
- Form BMC-84 (surety bond) or Form BMC-85 (trust fund), $75,000.
- Counterparty: surety company / trustee + FMCSA.
- Must remain continuously in effect; 30-day cancellation notice required.

**Step 3. File BOC-3 — Designation of Process Agents.** [REGULATED — 49 CFR §366.3, §366.4]
- Form BOC-3, one consolidated filing covering every state where the broker has an office or where it will write contracts.
- Brokers without CMVs may file BOC-3 on their own behalf (otherwise only a registered process-agent service may file).
- Counterparty: FMCSA + process-agent designees.
- Broker keeps one signed copy at principal place of business.

**Step 4. Vetting + 10-day protest period.** [REGULATED — 49 CFR §365.109]
- FMCSA publishes application in the FMCSA Register; 10 calendar days for any party to file a protest.
- Application may be flagged for "Vetting" (status "Suspended" in FMCSA systems) if compliance-history concerns arise.
- Counterparty: FMCSA, any third party with standing.

**Step 5. Operating Authority granted.** [REGULATED — 49 CFR §365.113]
- FMCSA issues broker *license* (distinct from carrier *certificate*); evidenced by publication in the FMCSA Register.
- Broker MAY NOT begin operations until this issuance is published.

**Step 6. (Brokers ONLY) — MCS-150 biennial update exemption.** [REGULATED — FMCSA guidance, 49 CFR §390.19T]
- Per current FMCSA guidance, brokers and freight forwarders are *exempt* from the MCS-150 biennial update.
- Brokers MUST report any change (name, address, ownership) via Form **MCSA-5889** when the change occurs.

### Phase B — Per-Transaction Workflow (every load)

**Step 7. Verify carrier authority + insurance.** [REGULATED implicitly — §371.2 definition; CUSTOM in mechanics]
- Counterparty: FMCSA SAFER / L&I public records.
- Regulation requires the broker to arrange transport "by an *authorized* motor carrier." How the broker verifies (SAFER lookup, L&I insurance check, third-party monitoring) is not specified.

**Step 8. Execute broker–carrier rate confirmation / dispatch.** [CUSTOM — §371 silent on document form]
- Captures fields that will populate the §371.3 record: carrier name/address/registration number; agreed line-haul rate; BOL number once issued.
- Counterparty: motor carrier.

**Step 9. Capture consignor + BOL information.** [REGULATED — 49 CFR §371.3(a)(1), (a)(3)]
- Record must show consignor name + address and BOL/freight-bill number.
- Counterparty: shipper / consignor.

**Step 10. Open the transaction record.** [REGULATED — 49 CFR §371.3(a), (b)]
- Record per shipment created the moment the brokered transaction occurs.
- Master lists of consignors/carriers permissible (no repetition).

**Step 11. Avoid prohibited conduct during the transaction.** [REGULATED — 49 CFR §371.7, §371.9]
- §371.7: broker may not operate under a name other than the registered name; may not represent itself as a carrier.
- §371.9(a): broker may not take carrier-side compensation when broker has a material beneficial interest in / owns / is owned by the shipper (no self-dealing).
- §371.9(b): broker may not give anything of value to shippers/consignors/consignees beyond inexpensive promotional items.
- §371.13: broker operating other businesses (incl. carrier ops) must keep brokerage accounts financially separate.

**Step 12. Carrier delivers; broker collects freight charges from shipper.** [CUSTOM document form, §371 silent on shipper payment instrument]
- Broker invoice issued.
- Counterparty: shipper.

**Step 13. Broker pays carrier; capture freight-charges-paid date.** [REGULATED — 49 CFR §371.3(a)(6)]
- Record must show "the amount of any freight charges collected by the broker and the date of payment to the carrier."
- Counterparty: motor carrier.

**Step 14. Capture broker compensation + non-brokerage service details.** [REGULATED — 49 CFR §371.3(a)(4), (a)(5)]
- Compensation received by broker + payer name (a)(4).
- Any non-brokerage service performed (e.g., assignment of insurance claim handling), the compensation received, and the payer (a)(5).

**Step 15. Honor right-to-review requests.** [REGULATED — 49 CFR §371.3(c)]
- Each party (shipper, carrier, broker) may request review of the §371.3 record for any transaction the requesting party was a party to.
- Pending FMCSA NPRM (Docket FMCSA-2023-0257, Nov 2024) would lock electronic format + 48-hour response window; not final.

### Phase C — Ongoing Record-Keeping + Compliance

**Step 16. Retain records 3 years.** [REGULATED — 49 CFR §371.3(d)]

**Step 17. Maintain surety bond ≥ $75,000 continuously.** [REGULATED — 49 CFR §387.307(d), (e)]
- Surety/trustee must notify FMCSA within 2 business days of any payout that reduces the bond below $75,000.
- Broker has 7 business days to cure (top up or settle claims) or operating authority is suspended.

**Step 18. Maintain BOC-3 process-agent designations.** [REGULATED — 49 CFR §366.4]
- Invalid BOC-3 → suspension of operating authority (FMCSA MC-RS-2019-0002).

**Step 19. Report changes via MCSA-5889 when they occur.** [REGULATED]
- Name, address, ownership changes must be filed.

## Part 3 — Gaps where regulation is silent vs. industry custom

| Topic | Regulated? | Industry custom fills the gap |
|---|---|---|
| Broker–carrier master contract format | **Silent** | TIA model agreement; bilateral master + per-load rate confirmation |
| Broker–shipper master contract format | **Silent** | Broker terms-of-service / master transportation services agreement |
| Carrier credit-history / payment-history check | **Silent** | Carrier411, MyCarrierPackets, Highway, Carrier Assure |
| Shipper credit check | **Silent** | Ansonia, RMS, Compunet, internal Net-30 underwriting |
| Carrier safety vetting beyond "authorized" | **Silent** | SAFER snapshot + SMS BASIC scores + crash record review |
| Insurance verification cadence | **Silent** | L&I daily/weekly monitoring services |
| Rate confirmation document content | **Silent** | Industry-standard form (origin, destination, commodity, weight, rate, payment terms) |
| Proof of delivery handling | **Silent** | POD passed shipper-side; broker retains for factoring/dispute |
| Detention/accessorial billing | **Silent** | Negotiated per-load; subject to §371.3(a)(5) non-brokerage-service recordkeeping if broker bills it |
| Dispute resolution forum | **Silent** | TIA arbitration / state court / federal court depending on contract |
| Proactive carrier-rate disclosure to shipper | **Silent (currently)** | §371.3(c) disclosure-on-request is the only federal hook; NPRM would tighten this |
