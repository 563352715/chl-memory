## 2.0 Federal preemption framework — 49 USC § 14501(b) and (c)

This is the load-bearing federal-law backdrop. Quoted from S1.

> **§ 14501(b)(1) — Freight forwarders and brokers:** "no State or political subdivision thereof and no intrastate agency or other political agency of 2 or more States shall enact or enforce any law, rule, regulation, standard, or other provision having the force and effect of law relating to intrastate rates, intrastate routes, or intrastate services of any freight forwarder or broker."

> **§ 14501(c)(1) — Motor carriers of property:** "a State, political subdivision of a State, or political authority of 2 or more States may not enact or enforce a law, regulation, or other provision having the force and effect of law related to a price, route, or service of any motor carrier ... or any motor private carrier, broker, or freight forwarder with respect to the transportation of property."

> **§ 14501(c)(2) — Preserved state authority** (safety carve-out): preemption does not "restrict the safety regulatory authority of a State with respect to motor vehicles, the authority of a State to impose highway route controls or limitations based on the size or weight of the motor vehicle ... or the authority of a State to regulate motor carriers with regard to minimum amounts of financial responsibility relating to insurance requirements ..." Also preserved: household goods transportation regulation, tow truck regulation with consent rules, and the Hawaii carve-out (S1, § 14501(b)(2)).

**Practical effect on CHL:**

1. Federal law preempts states from regulating intrastate **rates/routes/services** of brokers (§ 14501(b)(1) and (c)(1)). This means a state cannot dictate what CHL charges, where it routes, or what services it provides.
2. Federal law does **NOT** preempt: (a) state safety regulations applied to broker-arranged carriers, (b) state insurance/financial-responsibility minimums, (c) state household-goods regulation, (d) state taxation, (e) state business registration, and (f) certain license/registration regimes that survive as "safety" or "financial responsibility" regimes.
3. The line between "preempted rate/route/service regulation" and "permitted licensing/safety/insurance regulation" is **actively litigated**. The Supreme Court was hearing *Montgomery v. Caribe Transport II, LLC* in early 2026 on negligent-hiring preemption (S23). State broker license/bond regimes (TX, IL, CA-HHG, OR) have **not** been struck down as preempted as of 2026-05-09.
4. **Bottom line for CHL:** the state regimes cataloged below remain enforceable until courts strike them. CHL must comply with the licensing/bond/registration regimes of each state in which it brokers intrastate freight, even if § 14501 preemption is theoretically arguable.

### 2.2 Texas (TX) — DISTINCT INTRASTATE BROKER BOND REGIME

| Q | Answer |
|---|---|
| 1. Separate intrastate broker license? | **YES — sort of.** Texas does not issue a "license" but Tex. Trans. Code Ch. 646 imposes a mandatory **Intrastate Motor Transportation Broker Bond** on persons brokering freight wholly within Texas without FMCSA interstate authority. Brokers with FMCSA authority who broker intrastate Texas loads operate in a **gray zone** — § 646.003 by its terms applies regardless of FMCSA status; surety advisors (S21) state explicitly: "having an FMCSA bond or general liability coverage automatically satisfies state regulators—this leads to costly misunderstandings. Texas requires the separate state bond regardless of federal coverage." Direct TxDMV verification recommended before CHL's first TX-intrastate load. (S2, S7, S21) |
| 2. License fee | **No state filing fee** for the bond itself. Bond premium ~$200–$600/year (1–6% of $10K). (S21) |
| 3. State-level bond | **YES — $10,000 per Tex. Trans. Code § 646.003.** Separate from and additional to federal $75K BMC-84. (S2) |
| 4. State process agent | Not separately required for a brokerage filing this bond; bond payable to "Texas or a person to whom the motor transportation broker provides services." (S2) |
| 5. Insurance above federal | None broker-specific. |
| 6. Intrastate carrier vetting | TxDMV requires Form E (auto liability) and H/I (cargo) filings for intrastate **carriers**; CHL as broker must verify carriers it dispatches hold these. |
| 7. Tax obligations | Texas franchise tax (margin tax) applies if CHL has Texas nexus; sales tax on certain freight services is **exempt** under Tex. Tax Code § 151.0101 if separately stated. CHL must register with TX Comptroller if nexus established. |
| 8. Recent enforcement actions | Class C misdemeanor exposure under § 646.004 for unbonded intrastate brokerage. No high-profile broker enforcement actions in the 24-month window publicly reported. |
| 9. Reciprocity | **NO** — Texas does not waive § 646.003 for federally-registered brokers. |
| 10. Compliance triggers | Brokering any load with both origin AND destination in Texas. Single load triggers exposure if unbonded. Out-of-state broker arranging a TX intrastate load may be reached. |

**Near-term blocker if CHL takes a Texas intrastate load:** $10K state bond + TxDMV filing. Roughly $200–$600/yr premium + ~$50 administrative fee. Lead time: 2–4 weeks for surety underwriting.

### 2.3 California (CA) — STRICTEST OPERATING ENVIRONMENT

| Q | Answer |
|---|---|
| 1. Separate intrastate broker license? | **For PROPERTY brokers (CHL's authority): NO state broker license required.** CPUC's broker licensing regime ended for property brokers when household-goods authority migrated from CPUC to BHGS (Bureau of Household Goods and Services) effective 2018-07-01 (S14). CHL holds property authority only — HHG broker license not currently triggered. |
| 2. License fee | N/A property broker. HHG: $500. (S14, household movers application packet) |
| 3. State-level bond | **NONE for property brokers.** HHG: $2,000 C.O.D. bond + $15,000 subhaul-lease bond if using independent contractors. |
| 4. State process agent | Not separately required for property brokers. |
| 5. Insurance above federal | None broker-specific. Intrastate property **carriers** that CHL dispatches must hold a DMV-issued Motor Carrier Permit (MCP) with proof of insurance; CHL must verify. |
| 6. Intrastate carrier vetting (state-specific) | **YES — CARB compliance verification required.** "A California broker is any person or entity, physically located in or outside of California, that arranges for the transportation of goods or property into or within California by motor carriers with vehicles subject to the regulation. Verify via CARB TRUCRS database; record vehicle ID + CARB compliance status before dispatch. | 7. Tax obligations | California sales/use tax does NOT apply to most freight transportation services. California franchise tax ($800 minimum) if CHL has CA nexus. |
| 8. Recent enforcement actions | **YES — material.** CARB fined Marten Logistics $100,000 and Roadrunner Transportation $52,250 in 2018 for failing to verify carrier CARB compliance and maintain records (S16). Enforcement remains active 2026; CARB's broker fine schedule reaches **$10,000 per year** of non-compliant carrier hire. |
| 9. Reciprocity | **YES for property brokers** (federal authority sufficient for property-broker intrastate). **NO for CARB compliance** — broker physical location does not exempt; arranging transport of property "into or within California" triggers CARB obligations regardless of broker domicile. |
| 10. Compliance triggers | Any load with origin or destination in CA with a vehicle subject to CARB regulation = CARB broker obligations. CARB obligations broader than the strict-intrastate definition; out-of-state-broker arranging interstate-into-CA also triggers it. |

**Near-term blocker for CHL:** CARB broker compliance system. Build/extend carrier-vetting data store to capture CARB TRUCRS lookup result, vehicle ID, engine year, and date-of-verification per dispatched truck on any CA-touching load. This is a code/data deliverable, not a license filing.

## 8. Summary Findings

- **MO today-state:** GREEN — no specific intrastate broker rules CHL must comply with **today** beyond existing FMCSA authority + Missouri SOS LLC registration + Missouri DoR tax registration.
- **Federal preemption (49 USC § 14501) does not invalidate** the TX/IL/CA-CARB regimes as of 2026-05-09; these are framed as safety / financial-responsibility / licensing rules and survive preemption challenges to date.
